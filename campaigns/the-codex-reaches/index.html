The Codex Reaches now spans five regions, eight reviews, nine sessions. In the Southern Vales, the Covenant of Lanterns was sealed on the first afternoon — a pact that permits the Archive to watch every span of thread through those chambers while forbidding the speaking of any subject’s name. An amendment followed the same evening. Two margin-notes were struck and sealed. On the seventh review, Iolen Vespertine returned to those Vales under serious conditions and rewrote the entire compact of the region end-to-end — the boundary stones answering in sequence — but two seals were left loose in the margin when the work closed: an outer door that opens when it should hold, and a silent courier who sends travelers on without any roles at all.

Beyond the Southern Vales, three more regions came to the map in quick succession. At the Conductor’s Exchange, Mía Guajarde arrived uninvited as three roads converged on one gatehouse at once; she caught two correctness fractures before the mechanism finished its turn and held the gate sealed under serious mode. At the Harbor of Sigils above the Cistern of Monarch, Kadren Velloway inscribed a tenth channel and found two seals unfinished on inspection — the community ballot resolved quietly toward study. Kadren descended a second time the same day into the Scrivener’s Undercroft, where the blueprint was sound but two guardian posts had drifted and the new wing stood unarmored. The table chose to press deeper; the reckoning is still owed.

At the Courier’s Foundry, Theren Vask crossed the Thread-Marked Corridor and retired a family-forged lantern in favor of the Guild’s standard flame. Three blank stones were sent back to the cutter. The ballot at that threshold fell silent; the dice chose the doctrine of propagation. Iolen Vespertine then visited the Foundry’s outer road to amend a small waystone pact, setting the shrine’s lamp to burn by default. Two small coverage gaps were noted and the traveler was let through.

On the eighth review, a new warlock appeared at the outer gate of the Sealed Archive — the central vault of the Product Information realm, its records tended by Custos, whose wards decide who passes. Sael Mirethis came under serious conditions to fit a secondary key-slot beside the existing oath-token channel, easing the crossing for the v1 migration. The work arrived mid-cutover: one slot already in service, a second being fitted to the frame. The dice fell to seven, and two concerns blocked the passage — the raw key carried too far into the inner ledger, visible to any span-watcher on a denial, and no latch to close the secondary slot once the migration crossing was done. The party holds the gate while both are addressed. Eight reviews; ninety-two remain.

  • Southern Vales
    A realm dedicated to consent management and privacy. Its chambers handle subject data, and every lantern hung here is sworn to the Privacy Rule. Threads are watched by the Archive; no name is ever spoken. Home of Iolen Vespertine and the first pact of the campaign. The entire compact was renegotiated end-to-end on the seventh review; two clauses remain loose and are marked for the next draft.
  • The Conductor’s Exchange
    A sorting-house that indexes records keyed by language, audience, URL, and page type, then dispatches them to their rightful product surfaces. Three roads were being laid simultaneously when the campaign first visited. Home of Mía Guajarde. The gate is currently sealed pending two correctness blockers.
  • The Harbor of Sigils
    A vestibule-and-register above the lower Cistern of Monarch, where every contracted order presents its mark before its channel is opened into the flow. Nine rivulets meet beneath arched stone; a tenth has been freshly cut, and its seals are drying. Home of Kadren Velloway. The Scrivener’s Undercroft lies directly beneath — the record-vaults and schema chambers where the blueprints for each integration type are drawn and stored. A reckoning is owed there: the party chose to press deeper.
  • The Courier’s Foundry
    A forge-and-dispatch house where every change is stamped into a sigil-scroll, fed through a glass corridor, and handed to two couriers — one to the Salt-Queue, one to the Event-Bus — that the realm downstream might know what was writ. Home of Theren Vask. The Thread-Marked Corridor awaits three re-cut stones before it opens. Iolen Vespertine stopped at the border road to light the waystone shrine by default.
  • The Sealed Archive
    The central vault of the Product Information realm — the service that catalogs records and relations, and governs which callers may read or write its contents. Its outer gate is tended by Custos, whose wards decide who passes. Home of Sael Mirethis. A secondary key-slot is being fitted to the frame to ease the v1 migration crossing; two security concerns hold the gate while the warlock awaits resolution.
