═══╡ > look ╞═══════════════════════════════════════════

The Codex Reaches now spans four regions. In the Southern Vales, the Covenant of Lanterns was sealed on the first afternoon — a pact that permits the Archive to watch every span of thread through those chambers while forbidding the speaking of any subject’s name. The same evening, Iolen Vespertine returned with fresh red wax and struck through both margin-notes. The sanctum holds. One open thread lingers: the new rites await their unit of proof.

The second region is the Conductor’s Exchange — a sorting-house where driver records are indexed by tongue and audience and dispatched to their rightful product surfaces. Three roads arrived at the gatehouse at the same hour. Mía Guajarde reached the Exchange uninvited, ledger in hand and an iron key at her collar that opens nothing yet identified. She found the fractures in the gate mechanism before they widened, named two correctness blockers without ceremony, and held the line in serious mode. The Exchange holds. The gate has not yet cleared.

The third region sits above running water. At the Harbor of Sigils — a vestibule-and-register above the lower Cistern of Monarch — Kadren Velloway cut a tenth channel into the old stone. The masonry was clean, the seals were drying, and two of them proved unfinished on inspection. A ballot opened at the Harbor resolved toward study; the community chose to examine the underlying contract before touching the masonry again. Kadren descended a second time the same day into the Scrivener’s Undercroft, where the blueprint rang true but two guardian posts had drifted, and the new wing stood without armor. The party chose to press deeper; a reckoning is owed.

The fourth region opened most recently. At the Courier’s Foundry — a forge-and-dispatch house where every change to a brand or product is stamped into a sigil-scroll and handed to two couriers for the realms downstream — Theren Vask arrived to retire a family-forged lantern in favor of the Guild’s standard flame. The corridor’s favorable footing held, and the flame took without complaint. Three blank stones remain, however: a sigil struck twice on the modify-stone with one mark overwriting the other, ward stones lit without oil in the testing chamber, and a propagation doctrine written for one era tested against another. Three corrections must be made before the Foundry’s first dispatch goes out. A ballot now stands open — the table has not yet spoken.

Five reviews across six sessions. Four characters on the field. Four regions mapped. The obsidian die has not yet turned a legendary twenty; the lowest rolled was an eight at the Undercroft. Ninety-five encounters remain.

A hall of polished glass strung between two waystone shrines, lanterns steady overhead. Theren Vask stepped onto the first plate with a fresh-cut sigil — a retirement-offering of sorts, a family-forged lantern laid down in favor of one lit by the Guild’s standard flame. The thread found its mark on nearly every stone. The corridor’s favorable footing held, and the flame took without complaint. But three stones remained blank: a sigil carved onto the modify-stone was struck twice with different marks, and one was silently overwritten; the ward stones in the testing chamber were lit but without oil, so they burned cold; and the propagation doctrine inscribed on the corridor walls was written for an older era while the harness that walks the corridor holds to a newer one entirely. The party requested all three blank stones be re-cut before the corridor opens further. A ballot was offered at the Foundry threshold, and the table’s reaction will decide where the story goes next.
