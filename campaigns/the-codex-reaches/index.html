The Codex Reaches spans two regions now. In the Southern Vales, the Covenant of Lanterns was sealed on the first afternoon — a pact that allows the Archive to watch every span of thread through those chambers while forbidding the speaking of any subject’s name. The same evening, Iolen Vespertine returned with fresh red wax and struck through both margin-notes. The sanctum holds. One open thread lingers: the new rites await their unit of proof.

The second region is younger and louder. At the Conductor’s Exchange — a sorting-house where driver records are indexed by tongue and audience and dispatched to their rightful product surfaces — three roads arrived at the gatehouse simultaneously. Mía Guajarde reached the Exchange uninvited, ledger in hand and an iron key at her collar that opens nothing yet identified. She found the fractures in the gate mechanism before they widened, named two correctness blockers without ceremony, and held the line in serious mode. The Exchange holds. The gate has not yet been cleared.

Two reviews completed across three sessions. Two characters on the field. Two regions mapped. Ninety-eight encounters remain. The obsidian die has not yet turned up a legendary twenty; the darkest omen rolled was a nine — the Threefold Gate, in serious mode, still standing.
