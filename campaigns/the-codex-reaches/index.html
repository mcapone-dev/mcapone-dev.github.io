═══╡ > look ╞═══════════════════════════════════════════

The Codex Reaches spans three regions now. In the Southern Vales, the Covenant of Lanterns was sealed on the first afternoon — a pact that allows the Archive to watch every span of thread through those chambers while forbidding the speaking of any subject’s name. The same evening, Iolen Vespertine returned with fresh red wax and struck through both margin-notes. The sanctum holds. One open thread lingers: the new rites await their unit of proof.

The second region is the Conductor’s Exchange — a sorting-house where driver records are indexed by tongue and audience and dispatched to their rightful product surfaces. Three roads arrived at the gatehouse simultaneously. Mía Guajarde reached the Exchange uninvited, ledger in hand and an iron key at her collar that opens nothing yet identified. She found the fractures in the gate mechanism before they widened, named two correctness blockers without ceremony, and held the line in serious mode. The Exchange holds. The gate has not yet cleared.

The third region sits above running water. At the Harbor of Sigils — a vestibule-and-register above the lower Cistern of Monarch, where every contracted order presents its mark before its channel is opened into the flow — Kadren Velloway has cut a tenth channel into the old stone. The masonry is clean, the seals were drying, and two of them proved unfinished on inspection: a vestibule that promised nine doors and opened none, and a walker among the channels that could not name the new order when it met it. The torches held steady (🎲 11); the flow was not released. The seals will be recut.

Three reviews completed across four sessions. Three characters on the field. Three regions mapped. Ninety-seven encounters remain. The obsidian die has not yet turned up a legendary twenty; the darkest omen rolled was a nine — the Threefold Gate, in serious mode, still standing.
